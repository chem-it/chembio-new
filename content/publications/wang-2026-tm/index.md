abstract: Late-stage diversification of complex natural products enables rapid access
  to analogues for structure-activity relationship studies. Herein, we report a regioselective
  lactone-to-lactam editing strategy for bilobalide, affording previously inaccessible
  C-ring lactam analogues. The transformation proceeds through in situ activation
  of a benzoylated intermediate followed by cyclization, delivering fused bilobalide
  lactam scaffolds. This work expands the accessible chemical space of bilobalide
  for future biological exploration.
