abstract: A partial bioinspired as well as the total synthesis of archazolid F, a
  highly potent V-ATPase inhibitory, antiproliferative polyketide macrolide, is described.
  Key features of the synthetic routes include a highly stereoselective aldol condensation
  of two elaborate fragments and macrocyclizations either by a Shiina macrolactonization
  or by a challenging RCM reaction of an octaene substrate. The syntheses unequivocally
  confirm the full architecture of this very scarce archazolid.
